I've studied computer programming in one form or another since the fourth grade. I studied it on and off throughout my K-12 years. When I went off to the University of Missouri-Columbia in 1999, I went for a bachelor's in computer science. I ended up graduating with...a B.A. in Technical Theatre!?! I know, how did that happen? That's a story for another time, suffice it to say, it did happen, and I've spent my time since them working all over the country, with many different acts, at many different theaters. From the Santa Fe Opera, to the Alley Theatre in Houston, I ended up settling in Las Vegas. While here in Vegas I've worked for The Blue Man Group Cirque du Soliel and a few others in between. It's been pretty wild.

Well, that brings up to March 2020. I was at work, getting ready for another night in the Automation department at K; by Cirque du Soliel, when you guessed it, 2020 happened. Every show and casino on the strip, shuttered their doors and turned off the lights. We were the first ones to go, and if there are any shows still financially stable enough to reopen, we'll be the last ones to return.
